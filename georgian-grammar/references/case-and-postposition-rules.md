# Case And Postposition Rules

Use this file when checking cases, postpositions, possessive pronouns, reflexive possession, demonstratives, and direction forms.

## Decision Steps

1. Identify the relation first: location, direction, source, possession, reflexive ownership, comparison, accompaniment, agent, exclusion, topic, duration, or purpose.
2. Choose the governing case.
3. Add the postposition and apply the expected surface change.
4. Recheck possessive pronouns before dative or adverbial forms.
5. Recheck whether a pronoun is standing independently, modifying a noun, or replacing a noun phrase from earlier context.

Source-backed control: a postposition attaches to a definite case form; do not choose the surface ending by analogy alone.

## Common Postposition Governance

- dative-based: `-თან`, `-ზე`, `-ში`, `შორის`
- genitive-based: `-თვის`, `-გან`, `-კენ`, `-თანავე`, `-ებრ`, `მიმართ`, `მიერ`, `გარდა`, `შესახებ`, `განმავლობაში`
- instrumental-based: `-ურთ`
- nominative or expanded dative: `-ვით`
- adverbial-based: `-მდე`

Surface effects:

- with `-ზე` and `-ში`, the dative `ს` usually disappears: `სახლზე`, `სახლში`
- with `-თან`, the dative `ს` may disappear before difficult consonant clusters: `კართან`, `სახლთან`
- for source-person or source-thing questions, use genitive plus `-გან`; for source-place or source-time questions, use the `-დან/-იდან` type.

## Possessive Pronouns

Before dative or adverbial noun forms, use the possessive form that agrees with the case relation.

- prefer: `ჩემს მეგობარს`
- prefer: `შენს მეგობრად`
- prefer: `ჩვენს ქალაქს`
- prefer: `თქვენს მეუღლეს`
- prefer: `მათს მეგობარს`

Avoid forms such as `ჩემი მეგობარს` or `თქვენი მეუღლეს`.

## Reflexive Possession

For third-person ownership, first decide whether the owner is the current subject.

- Use `თავისი` for the subject's own possession: `ნინომ თავისი მანქანა გაყიდა`.
- Use `თავიანთი` when the owners are plural and the possession belongs to them.
- Use `მისი` or `მათი` when the owner is another third person, not the subject of the clause.
- If `თავისი` and `მისი` would both sound possible but point to different owners, rewrite with the owner's name instead of leaving the sentence ambiguous.

Do not translate English "his/her/their" mechanically. Georgian often requires the reflexive possessive where English does not mark it.

## Pronoun Reference

Before accepting a pronoun, identify its referent and syntactic job.

- If the pronoun stands alone, inflect the pronoun itself for the sentence relation.
- If the pronoun modifies an explicit noun, match the pronoun form to that noun phrase instead of treating it as an independent pronoun.
- If the pronoun replaces a noun from earlier context, make sure the person/thing distinction and number are still clear.
- If two antecedents are possible, rewrite with the noun or name rather than leaving an ambiguous pronoun.
- In stylistic tasks, route heavy possessive-pronoun phrases to `style-and-lexical-choice.md` when a concise Georgian lexical equivalent may express the relation better.

## Demonstratives In Cases

When a demonstrative modifies a noun, the stem changes before the case-marked noun:

- `ეს სახლი` -> `ამ სახლმა`, `ამ სახლს`, `ამ სახლის`, `ამ სახლით`
- `ეგ სახლი` -> `მაგ სახლმა`, `მაგ სახლს`, `მაგ სახლის`, `მაგ სახლით`
- `ის სახლი` -> `იმ სახლმა`, `იმ სახლს`, `იმ სახლის`, `იმ სახლით`

When the demonstrative stands alone as a pronoun, use the full inflected forms:

- `ეს` -> `ამან`, `ამას`, `ამის`, `ამით`
- `ეგ` -> `მაგან`, `მაგას`, `მაგის`, `მაგით`
- `ის` -> `იმან`, `იმას`, `იმის`, `იმით`

Do not leave `ეს/ეგ/ის` unchanged before an inflected noun when the case requires the `ამ/მაგ/იმ` stem.

## Additional Postposition Controls

- `შორის` marks "between/among" or an interval and normally follows dative-marked nouns or coordinated dative forms: `სახლებს შორის`, `საუზმესა და სადილს შორის`.
- `შუა` or `შუაში` points to the central place inside an area; do not use it as a synonym for every `შორის` relation.
- `მიმართ` takes a genitive base for attitude or relation toward someone or something: `ადამიანების მიმართ`.
- `მიერ` takes a genitive base for an actor/agent, especially with passive or result-oriented wording: `კომპანიის მიერ ჩატარებული`.
- `გარდა` takes a genitive base for exclusion or "besides": `კოტეს გარდა`.
- `შესახებ` takes a genitive base for topic/aboutness and can often be recast with `-ზე`: `წიგნის შესახებ`, `წიგნზე`.
- `განმავლობაში` takes a genitive time expression for duration: `ორი წლის განმავლობაში`.

## Direction With `-კენ`

Use the standard directional pronoun forms:

- `ჩემკენ`
- `შენკენ`
- `ჩვენკენ`
- `თქვენკენ`

Avoid adding the possessive `-ს` before `-კენ` in these pronoun forms.

## Brand Names and Latin Script Declension

When brand, model, or entity names remain in English/Latin script (e.g., Google, OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek), Georgian case suffixes are usually attached with a hyphen. Do not treat this as a reason to keep every name in Latin script: in ordinary Georgian prose, a natural Georgian transliteration may be better.

- **Nominative (-ი)**: Use when the name needs a nominative Georgian ending.
  - `Google-ი`, `Apple-ი`, `DeepSeek-ი`
- **Ergative (-მა)**: Use when Georgian syntax requires an ergative-marked subject, most often in aorist-series transitive constructions.
  - `OpenAI-მა`, `Claude-მა`, `DeepSeek-მა`
- **Dative (-ს)**: Use for dative-marked objects, recipients, and many postpositional structures.
  - `OpenAI-ს`, `Google-ს`, `DeepSeek-ს`
- **Genitive (-ის)**: Use for possession, relation, or noun-modifying structures.
  - `Claude-ის`, `OpenAI-ის მოდელი`
- **Instrumental (-ით)**: Use when acting via or using the brand/model. If the result sounds clumsy, prefer a clearer phrase with `მეშვეობით`.
  - `Gemini-ით`, `Llama-თი`, `Gemini-ს მეშვეობით`
- **Adverbial (-ად)**: Use only when the meaning is genuinely "as/into".
  - `OpenAI-ად`
